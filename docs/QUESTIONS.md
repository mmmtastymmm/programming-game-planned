## Open

- **Q35 (Terrain — Sand).** Sand as a heavy-cost tile (~5×, tuning constant). Open: does it need a distinguishing hook beyond cost, since Mud already owns "slow tile" (3×, 4× loaded)? Candidate: **sand punishes stopping** — idling more than N ticks on sand sinks the bot (escalating exit cost or a `stuck` fault), so `wait(n)` staging and rally points become unsafe in dunes. Mud punishes hauling routes; sand would punish loitering.
- **Q36 (Terrain — Mountain edge costs).** Should movement cost become a function of the **edge** (`from → to`) instead of the destination tile? Proposal: climbing onto a mountain is expensive, descending moderately expensive, ridge-to-ridge is plains-speed — mountain ranges become highways with costly on-ramps. Interacts with planned **High Ground** (ramp-gated, +2 sensor): do soft-climb mountains replace it (keeping the sensor bonus on summits) or coexist as the harder variant? Note: edge costs touch A*/`move_to`, so golden replay hashes change when this lands.
- **Q37 (Terrain — Ice).** Slide tile: entering ice continues the move in the same direction until non-ice (deterministic, 1×/tile, uncontrolled). Programs must plan slide endpoints; mass-produces `on bump:` use. Open: how do slides interact with bump freezes and one-way arrow overlays mid-slide?
- **Q38 (Terrain — Ford).** Shallow water: passable at high cost (~4×), possibly sensor-silent while wading. Makes bridges an upgrade rather than a hard gate — smooths the pre-terraforming game. Open: does this undercut the bridge/chokepoint economy that Water is designed to create?
- **Q39 (Terrain — Roads + integer cost scaling).** Terraformed road tile at half plains cost (the "tech tile" ground art). Requires scaling the base cost table ×2 (Plains 2, Road 1, Rubble 4, ...) since costs are integers — a one-time costs.ron migration that also buys finer tuning granularity for everything else. Decide before more cost-sensitive terrain ships.
- **Q40 (Terrain — wear & traces).** Two related "terrain remembers traffic" ideas: **Scree** that collapses after N crossings (per-tile counter, like natural bridge HP) so optimal programs rotate routes; and **footprints on sand** — decaying marks a future `tracks_at()` sensor could read, turning scouting from "where are they" into "where were they." Open: is per-tile mutable traffic state acceptable sim-size-wise, and is `tracks_at()` a Tier-2 sensor or later?
- **Q44 (Quirks — ship it? distribution).** Do quirks ([09-quirks.md](09-quirks.md)) ship at all, and behind what dial? Candidate: a match setting (Off / Rare / Standard), with Standard weighting most prints to 1 quirk and a minority to 0 or 2. Quirks change replay hashes, so they land behind the setting regardless.
- **Q45 (Quirks — acquisition mix).** Print-roll, scars-on-rescue, L5 track merits, corruption exposure: which subset makes v1? Print-roll alone is the cheapest to build; scars/merits do the most for pillar 3 but touch the rescue and XP systems.
- **Q46 (Quirks — removal/reroll).** Can a Repair Bay (or a pricier "Refit Bay") remove a scar or reroll a print quirk for resources? A cheap remove cheapens scars; no remove at all may make heavily-scarred veterans feel worse than reprinting, which fights the rescue incentive.
- **Q47 (Quirks — enemy visibility).** Are an enemy bot's quirks readable for free (visible wear/decals, like XP levels per pillar 2), gated behind that color's decryption %, or split (physical quirks visible, internal quirks like Memory Leak gated)?
- **Q48 (Quirks — introspection unlock).** `my_quirks()` / `has_quirk()`: free from the start (quirks are self-evident to their owner) or a cheap early function block? If quirks ship in a match where nobody can read them from code, they're pure noise — the builtin may need to be free whenever the setting is on.
- **Q53 (Agents — where does base sensor range live?).** The stat sheet ([02-agents.md](02-agents.md)) gives every bot an innate universal base sensor range (5 tiles). Alternative: sight is a tool module ("Optics") competing for slots — blindness becomes a build choice, but with the universal 1-slot floor that tax is now the *whole* build. Or hybrid: innate base, module extends. Interacts with High Ground (+2), the Scouting track, and Q66 (slot growth). *(2026-07-14: the Sand → Glass → Lens chain ([03-resources.md](03-resources.md)) now supplies the material — whatever form Optics takes, it's built from Lenses.)*
- **Q54 (Agents — signature/detectability stat).** Formalize "enemies sense this bot at ±N" (Loud Fans, Promiscuous Mode) as a **signature** stat: bot B is perceived by enemy E at `E.sensor_range + B.signature`. Requires asymmetric sensing in fog/vision code — perceiving and being perceived stop being the same radius. Worth the sim complexity, or should detectability effects be cut until a stealth system earns them? *(2026-07-13: the Hiding body track now gives signature its growth story — levels by being seen, [02-agents.md](02-agents.md) — so if signature ships, it ships leveling.)*
- **Q55 (Agents — does the countdown explosion deal damage?).** With explosions now happening only at countdown expiry, is the blast cosmetic (the bot is simply gone) or an area-damage event? Area damage makes deliberate scuttles a weapon (the Hanged Man's ticking-wreck doorstep bombs, [04-enemies.md](04-enemies.md)) and makes clearing enemy wrecks urgent — but it also punishes standing near your *own* dying bots and adds a griefing vector on non-PvP servers (wreck-walk bombing). If yes: damage/radius scale with max HP? with cargo?
- **Q57 (Agents/Terrain — sensor range: reveal vs. query, per-kind bonuses).** Fog is eyes-only *tile* reveal, but Combat L3 grants "+1 sensor range vs enemies" and the Ore-acle merit "+2 for resource nodes" ([09-quirks.md](09-quirks.md)) — a per-kind bonus can't reveal a tile "only for enemies." Ruling needed: is sensor range simultaneously the fog-reveal radius and the query radius (`closest`/`exists`/`scan_*`)? Do per-kind bonuses extend queries only, and may a query return an entity standing on an unrevealed tile? Interacts with High Ground (+2), Q38's sensor-silent wading, and Q54's signature stat.
- **Q58 (Language — env visibility to enemies).** Are a foreign bot's env values readable for free (like XP decals — you can see an enemy hauler runs `hurt_line=70`), gated behind that color's decryption %, or fully private? Free reading rewards attention and fits pillar 2; but `hurt_line` is exactly the number an attacker wants (burst it past the line before the handler fires), so free env may make custom thresholds self-defeating.
- **Q59 (Language — user-defined env keys).** V1 env keys are engine-defined only. Arbitrary user keys would be genuine bot-local *persistent memory* (state surviving restarts — which the variables-clear-on-restart rule deliberately forbids). Powerful and dangerous: enables stateful veterans and colony protocols, weakens the "re-derive your state" discipline, and gives fault loops a way to persist corrupted state. Never? Late unlock? Capped key count as a hardware stat (Memory bank)?
- **Q60 (Quirks — policy quirks via env: temperament vs. compulsion).** Policy quirks are implemented against the env registry ([09-quirks.md](09-quirks.md)): a **temperament** shifts a key's default (one `setenv` overrides it — the quirk evaporates under a good dotfile) while a **compulsion** clamps the key's range (permanent — tune the fear, can't remove it). Which strength for which quirk? And does `setenv` past a quirk clamp **clip** quietly (proposed — one program stays valid on every bot; `getenv` reports the landing value) or **fault** (louder, but the same line faults on one bot and not its twin)? Note scars probably *want* to be compulsions — a temperament scar is weightless on any colony with boot-window dotfiles.
- **Q61 (Language — module semantics & limits).** Modules ([01-language.md](01-language.md)) formalize the Tier-4 program library. Open: unlock placement (bundled with `def`, or a separate construct right after it?); do imported lines count against per-bot **program memory** (a fat library raises every importing color's hardware bar — Q52's now-answered mechanism: over-bar members drop to the remainder) or is the closure exempt and only caps on module count/size apply?; can allies be granted modules the way they're granted channels (the co-op "shared library" scaffolding in [08-multiplayer.md](08-multiplayer.md) suggests yes)? *Update 2026-07-13:* import syntax + deploy-time resolution are DECIDED and live in the sandbox (Python's two forms; artifacts inline editor-generated `module <name>:` blocks; unknown module / unknown member / unimported qualified call / alias collisions are deploy errors; modules hold `def`s only for now — module `enum`s still to come). Still open besides the above: function-granularity tree-shaking (the sandbox ships whole imported modules).
- **Q63 (Rendering — adaptive terrain art / autotiling).** Water autotiles in v1: each water tile picks one of 16 textures from a NESW same-neighbor bitmask (bank overlays appear against land; Bridge counts as water so the river visibly flows under the planks; off-map counts as water so edge rivers run clean off the board). Pure view layer — no sim state, no replay exposure. Queued ideas: an 8-bit blob set (47 variants) for seamless inner corners; **dual-grid marching squares for grass** so patches read as rounded organic shapes; variants keyed on same-neighbor *count* (denser tufts deep inside a field); a deterministic per-position hash picking among equivalent variants so large fields don't look stamped; subtle flow lines along long straight river runs. Open: which technique grass gets (blob set vs dual-grid), and whether the dual-grid's half-tile-offset render quads fight the slab-per-tile scene setup.
- **Q64 (Printers — target-share allocation edges).** Narrowed twice 2026-07-13: any stat can be keyed; rule edits set the printer priority order; re-allocation runs on rule edits and on a player-set **check interval** (every X ticks, default 1000 — which also answers recall pacing: reassigned bots recall at the check, all at once, on a clock the player owns). Still open. **Key direction**: does a key always take the *best* by the stat's improvement direction, or can a printer ask for worst-first (e.g. a cheap-labor color that claims the lowest-XP rookies)? **Composite keys** (sort by X, tie-break by Y before entity ID)? **Percentage base**: % of the fleet *cap* (spec'd) or of the live fleet size?
- **Q67 (Terrain — Snow mechanic).** Snow shipped 2026-07-13 as a tile kind + art (autotiled melt fringe) at plains cost — pure scenery so far, which violates the "every terrain type must change what a good program looks like" rule ([05-terrain.md](05-terrain.md)) until it gets a hook. Candidates: mild slow (2×, but Rubble owns that); **conceals what stands still** (stationary bots sink from enemy sensors after N ticks — the ambush tile, inverse of Q35's sand punishing loitering); or the natural home of Q40's footprint traces (`tracks_at()` reads freshest in snow). Pick one before a snow biome ships on a real map.
- **Q65 (Printers — do ghost fleets survive the remainder bucket?).** Under desired-max dials, a dormant printer's stragglers could deliberately be left un-absorbed as a **ghost fleet** (frozen code, dying by attrition — with the permanent-intel wrinkle in [01-language.md](01-language.md)). The remainder bucket auto-claims every unclaimed bot, which force-marches stragglers home through whatever just killed their nest — recall is an interrupt context, so that trip can be a death march. Keep auto-absorb (simple, but deletes the ghost-fleet play), add a per-printer "abandon" toggle on dormancy, or make dormant stragglers claimable-but-never-auto-absorbed?
- **Q68 (Agents — use-based growth edges).** The body tracks ([02-agents.md](02-agents.md)) level by events happening to the bot, which invites **deliberate farming**: parade before enemy sensors to level Hiding, crash-loop to level Flinch, boot-cycle to level Boot, walk laps to level Mileage. (Age is farm-proof — time passes regardless, and idling still pays upkeep.) Emergent charm (grinding costs real time, resources, and risk) or exploit needing caps/diminishing returns per source? **Print-XP residence**: XP dies with the bot but a reprint is a *new* bot — if Print XP dies too, the track can never level. Live on the blueprint/lineage? The Fabricator? Or the one death-proof track? **Upgrade Station mechanics**: does an upgrade take time on the pad (physical queue, vulnerable — can enemies deny compute by camping the station)? Cumulative cost curve per bot? **Derived formulas**: what exactly do salvage profile and upkeep sum over, and are they enemy-visible (pillar 2 suggests a juicy corpse should *look* juicy)?
- **Q69 (Resources — raw/refined edges).** The raw/refined split ([03-resources.md](03-resources.md)) leaves edges open. *(Narrowed twice 2026-07-14: **Stone** added as the tenth raw — fortification & civil works; **Sand → Glass → Lens** added as the eleventh raw + seeing chain, giving Q53's Optics a material story. Sand-the-resource sits on shoreline flats; Q35's dune *terrain* remains its own question.)* **Kind constants**: Pyrite's `ore` constant should stay as the *family* query (`closest(ore)` = nearest mineable vein of any kind, keeping every existing example valid) with per-raw constants (`iron`, `coal`, …) added — confirm, and decide whether `mine()` yields report the specific kind. **Recipes & rates**: all recipe ratios, Wood-vs-Coal burn rates, coolant per compute upgrade, and Exchange rates per resource (Gold anchor) are unplaytested tuning. **Map generation**: guaranteed start-zone Iron+Coal+Wood+Stone, Copper/Tin within first-expansion reach, tier ladder laid out center-out; Tin sparsity needs a floor or Bronze soft-locks. **Salvage composition**: wrecks now contain Steel + Bronze + Chips-worth of material — does `salvage()` return the mix or just Steel? **Migration**: the sim currently implements the old Ore/Metal chain — renaming touches `mine()`, node types, costs.ron, and replay hashes.

- **Q51**: **window-reachable code is straight-line + `if`, all the way down** — user `def`s derive signal-safety (safe iff all calls safe, no loops, no recursion), each safe def gets a deploy-computed worst-case instruction count, and window calls charge that worst case against the cap.
